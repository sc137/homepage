Teaching Philosophy

My classroom is an environment built for learning and exploration. My classroom is energetic, academically rigorous, and open for input. Students are encouraged to "jump in" with their ideas at almost any time. Whether my class is taught in person, or online, students are encouraged to participate in the conversation and to "find their own voice" during learning activities.

I model my activities and assignments from research-proven techniques such as multiple low-stake assessments, pair sharing, relevant and timely feedback on student assignments, active learning activities, and many more. I look to the Universal Design for Learning and have studied pedagogy through On Course, Project-Based Learning, and USC Equity Now.

I encourage students to push themselves academically while maintaining achievable goals for all students to be able to succeed. I design activities that take into account cultural relevancy and students' own lived experiences.

My students are treated as humans and equals. In my classroom, we are all people striving toward similar goals, and it's only in this moment that I am the one standing in front and guiding. I encourage my students to share their own experience and expertise in the classroom. I model how their expertise builds the experience for the students. We look to each other to fill in the gaps.

My classroom is a place of open wonder and query. We seek answers together. I model growth mindset as way to reach higher levels of achievement.

My students succeed in an equitable and inclusive environment. My very high success rates have shown over the years that students are successful in my classroom across both race and gender boundaries.

Sable Cantus, M.A., CISSP
